# Trademark Policy — TigerTag & Tiger Studio

## TL;DR

- ✅ You can **use, fork, and redistribute** this software freely under the MIT License.
- ✅ You can **build commercial products** on top of Tiger Studio's code.
- ✅ You can say your app is **"based on Tiger Studio"** or **"compatible with TigerTag"**.
- ❌ You cannot name your product or fork **"Tiger Studio"** without authorization.
- ❌ You cannot use the **"TigerTag"** name for a competing RFID protocol or cloud service.

---

## Protected trademarks

### "TigerTag"

**"TigerTag"** is a trademark of the TigerTag Project.

TigerTag refers to the **RFID spool-tracking protocol** and the **cloud service**
(`tigertag.io`) that synchronizes filament inventory across devices.

You **may** reference "TigerTag" in a factual, descriptive way
(e.g. *"compatible with TigerTag"*, *"works with the TigerTag cloud"*) without permission.

You **may not** use "TigerTag" as the name of a competing RFID protocol,
cloud service, or product brand without explicit written authorization.

---

### "Tiger Studio"

**"Tiger Studio"** is a trademark of the TigerTag Project.

Tiger Studio refers to the **official desktop inventory management application**
published at [github.com/TigerTag-Project/TigerTag_Studio_Manager](https://github.com/TigerTag-Project/TigerTag_Studio_Manager).

You **may** fork the Tiger Studio source code (MIT License) and build your own application.

You **may not** name your fork or derivative application **"Tiger Studio"**,
**"TigerTag Studio"**, or any name confusingly similar to "Tiger Studio"
without explicit written authorization.

#### Forking — "Powered by Tiger Studio" is required

If you fork Tiger Studio and distribute it under your own name, displaying
**"Powered by Tiger Studio"** is **mandatory**. This is a condition of using
the Tiger Studio codebase, alongside the MIT copyright notice requirement.

**Required attribution forms** (pick one):
- `Powered by Tiger Studio`
- `Built on Tiger Studio`

**Where it must appear** (at minimum one of):
- The app's **About screen** or credits dialog
- A persistent **footer or sidebar** visible in the main UI
- The app's **README and store listing** (App Store, GitHub, website)

**The attribution must include a link** to
`https://github.com/TigerTag-Project/TigerTag_Studio_Manager`
or `https://tigertag.io` wherever the format supports hyperlinks.

**Ready-to-use badge** — drop this in your README:

```markdown
[![Powered by Tiger Studio](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/TigerTag-Project/TigerTag_Studio_Manager/main/assets/badges/powered-by-tiger-studio.svg)](https://github.com/TigerTag-Project/TigerTag_Studio_Manager)
```

Or in HTML:
```html
<a href="https://github.com/TigerTag-Project/TigerTag_Studio_Manager">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/TigerTag-Project/TigerTag_Studio_Manager/main/assets/badges/powered-by-tiger-studio.svg" alt="Powered by Tiger Studio">
</a>
```

**Valid fork examples:**
- *3DFilament — Powered by Tiger Studio*
- *FilaTrack, built on Tiger Studio*

No approval needed — just use a distinct app name and display the attribution.
Want your fork listed on our ecosystem page? Open an issue.

## The MIT License and trademark are separate

The MIT License governs the **source code** — it allows you to use, modify,
copy, and distribute the software, including commercially, with no royalties.

The trademark governs the **names and brand** — it ensures that products
called "Tiger Studio" or "TigerTag" come from or are authorized by the TigerTag Project.

You can have MIT rights to the code and still be bound by trademark rules on the name.
This is standard practice in open-source (Arduino, OpenWrt, Prusa Research, etc.).
