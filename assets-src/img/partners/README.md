# Partner packaging — filament makers shipping TigerTag

Photographs of retail boxes from filament manufacturers whose spools carry the **TIGER TAG RFID**
badge. These are the only assets in the project that prove *adoption* rather than describing the
product — a render shows what TigerTag is, these show that companies already ship it.

Masters only, at full resolution. **Nothing here is used anywhere yet**, and no optimised copy has
been produced under `assets/`, because the use is not decided. When one is (an explainer, a partner
page, a press kit), produce the shipped copy then and mirror its name back here, per the
`assets-src/` convention.

Two variants of each shot, both worth keeping:

- **cut-out** — transparent background, drops onto any layout or colour. The one to reach for in an
  explainer, a lineup, or over a coloured section.
- **studio** — soft grey backdrop, twice the resolution. The one for a standalone image or print.

The set will grow as more manufacturers adopt the badge; keep the `box-<brand>-<variant>` pattern so
it stays sortable.

## ⚠️ Before publishing any of these

These photographs show **other companies' trademarks and trade dress** — eSun, SUNLU and ROSA3D
(whose mark is registered, per the ® on its box). Two separate points:

- **Factual use is normally fine**: showing that a manufacturer's product carries the TigerTag badge
  is a true statement about a real product, and referring to a brand to describe reality is generally
  permitted.
- **Implying endorsement or partnership is not.** A page reading "our partners" over these three
  logos claims a commercial relationship. If that relationship exists, say so and keep the written
  permission; if it does not, the wording has to stay factual — "filament makers shipping TigerTag"
  rather than "our partners".

Ask each manufacturer for written permission before any prominent marketing use. It is usually
granted readily — a maker who put your badge on their packaging has already chosen to be associated
with it — and it costs nothing to have on file. Not legal advice; see
`TigerTag-RFID-Guide/docs/TRADEMARK-FILING.md` for the project's own trademark position.
